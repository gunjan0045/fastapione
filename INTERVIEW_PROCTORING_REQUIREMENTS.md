# Interview Proctoring Requirements

## 1. Multiple Persons Detection

During the interview session, only one person should be visible on screen at any given time. If the system detects more than one person in the video feed, a warning notification must be displayed to the candidate. If the candidate fails to resolve the issue within a reasonable timeframe, the interview should automatically terminate to ensure the integrity of the assessment.

## 2. Candidate Absence / Camera Off / Pause Detection

If the candidate moves away from the camera, turns off the camera, or pauses the interview for an extended period, the system must automatically detect these actions and stop the interview session. The candidate should not be able to resume the interview after such detection; the session must be ended immediately to prevent cheating or unauthorized assistance.

## 3. Suspicious Behavior Detection

During the interview, the system must monitor for suspicious behaviors including, but not limited to:
- The candidate talking on a phone or cell phone
- The candidate talking to another person in the room
- The candidate making unusual or excessive head movements
- The candidate using a mobile device or smartphone

If any of these behaviors are detected, a warning notification should be displayed to the candidate. If the suspicious behavior persists even after the warning has been issued, the interview should automatically stop to maintain the security and fairness of the assessment process.
